Transportation for YOU!

What is the Problem?

YOU are getting LATE for SCHOOL.
Your MOM and DAD pick YOU up LATE.
MOM and DAD get LATE to WORK.

Personal Motivations

The main reason why I chose to solve this problem is that my parents sometimes treat me like a chofer. Let it be early in the morning or late at night in a weekend, there are times where my parents can't drive my little sister and I have to do it for them. My parent's don't trust Uber with my little sister, and public transportation is not even an option in Mexico. Another reason why I want to solve this problem is the amount of times I hear complaints from relatives about this problem. Every weekend at our family gatherings they ask how is it possible that given the technology available to us there is still no solution to safe transportation for children.

Formal Solutions Previously Created

One of the most popular solutions for this conflict is the school bus. Some schools in Mexico try to implement a bus system for their school, but many times this fails. Mexican parents are very protective about their children, and some of them are doubtful about sending their children in a bus. Another formal solution is using Uber's transportation service, but it may require an adult to accompany the children as no one would send a 5 year old with a chofer they don't personally know.

Interviews

The process of interviewing a selective group of our user group was very straightforward. We brought up the problem with a parent, and then we would not ask any questions. Rather, we would let the interviewee talk about the subject and hear their proposals to solutions. Most importantly, we would listen to the user's requirements. Interviews with parents (our user group) gave some very interesting facts about the problem. Parents are willing to pay a higher price for safe and trustworthy transportation rather than using a cheaper alternative such as Uber. Parents value their time and would prefer to have someone trustworthy drive their children to school or other activities rather than doing it themselves. Another important fact gathered from the interview was the parent's emphasis on safety. They want to be able to know where their children is and where at ALL TIMES!

Part 2

Solutions

Solution 1: Building a bus transportation system just for kids.
Viability: Not viable. This requires us to own buses, to train bus drivers and hire them, we need to fuel the buses and create routes to pick up children all around the city.

Solution 2: Implementing an Uber-like application just for children.
Viability: Viable. If we can somehow restrict the chofers by having very detailed and specific entry requirements, we can create a safe transportation for children.

Solution 3: Train and rent chofers.
Viability: Viable. We can select people we trust and train then in childcare. Ideal case would be for chofers to supply their own cars, worst case would be having to supply cars form them which is not viable.

Solution 4: Implement a system of carpool with other parents.
Viability: Viable. If we can precisely select a group of children who share similar travel routes, we could fit three kids in a single car and that would take only one adult to drive them instead of three.

Solution 5: Move to a place close to school.
Viability: It is not viable as not every person can afford to a new place. Also, this would imply that there are residences near every school and this is not always the case.

Best Solution

The solution chosen was implementing an Uber-like application just for children. With the rigurous filters applied to selecting chofers for the platform, a parent could trust his kid will be in good hands. Also, a parent will be able to track their children's location by utilizing the gps in the driver's phone. Payment would be charged automatically so there is no need for the children to carry money. Trips would be on demand, so there is no need to pay for a full time chofer.

Metodology

Once we have the idea of our product, we have to study our clientele group. We need to know what they want, what they require, and what they think about the product. Conduct surveys, interviews, and a research on the study group. Once we know everything about our user, we must create a mockup to see how our users react to it. We need to get active feedback in order to modify the requirements and adjust it to the client's need. After an initial testing of scenarios, we would start to build a prototype. It involves more functionality, with constant testing and feedback from the user group. Here is where the iterative design phase comes into play. We would design, test it with the users and modify the product until a proper product is built.
